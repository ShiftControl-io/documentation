## Overview

If your organization runs on Google Workspace, you can set up ShiftControl yourself. Sign up with your Google Workspace account, validate your domain, authorize the directory, and land on a working ShiftControl org with your users, groups, and admin roles already imported.

If you also run JumpCloud (alone or alongside Google), the [guided onboarding path](/onboarding/Onboarding-intro) is a better fit. JumpCloud setup involves trust steps in the Google Admin console that are easier to walk through together.

<Admonition type="info" title="What you'll need">
    To finish self-service setup, you'll need: (1) a Google Workspace account in the organization you want to manage, (2) a Google Workspace **Super Admin** who can approve the OAuth authorization — that can be you, or someone you delegate the OAuth step to via a 48-hour link, and (3) a domain that isn't already claimed by another ShiftControl organization.
</Admonition>

## How It Works

Setup is a five-step wizard. Most of the work happens behind the scenes while you click through:

1. **Sign-up** — You sign in with your Google Workspace account. ShiftControl creates a user record for you and links it to your Google identity. Any Workspace user can do this step.
2. **Domain validation** — ShiftControl confirms that your email's domain belongs to a Google Workspace, and that no other ShiftControl organization has already claimed it. This prevents two organizations from claiming the same workspace.
3. **Authorization** — A Google Workspace Super Admin grants ShiftControl permission to manage your directory. If that's you, you'll see Google's approval screen right after domain validation. If it isn't, you can send your Super Admin a 48-hour authorization link and pause here until they approve it. The [Google Workspace OAuth flow](/using-shiftcontrol/Integrations/guides/Google-Workspace-OAuth-flow/) page has the technical detail on every permission requested.
4. **Connect apps (optional)** — The wizard offers app connectors (HRIS, etc.). You can skip everything here and add them later from Settings.
5. **Provisioning** — Once the OAuth is approved, ShiftControl reads your Google directory, creates the org records, sets Google Workspace as your primary directory, and imports your roles, groups, and users. You land on the Manage Users screen when it's done.

Provisioning happens in a single transactional step — if anything fails partway, the system rolls back so you don't end up half-onboarded. Once provisioning succeeds, completed organizations are gated from returning to the onboarding flow.

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario: First IT hire at a 30-person startup

You're the new IT person at a company that started on Google Workspace. There's no JumpCloud, no Okta — just Google. You want centralized control over users, groups, and app access this week, not next month. Self-service onboarding gets you a working ShiftControl org in under ten minutes. You import every existing Workspace user automatically, create your first group, and start cleaning up SaaS access immediately.

### Scenario: Founder evaluating ShiftControl before talking to sales

You're a CEO who wants to see ShiftControl working with your real data before scheduling a demo. You sign up self-service, look at your actual users and apps, and decide whether the product fits — without committing to a meeting first.

### Scenario: Replacing an old IdP

Your company is moving off another identity provider and consolidating on Google Workspace. Self-service onboarding gives you a clean ShiftControl org on top of Google so you can rebuild your group and app access model from scratch.

## Step-by-Step Guide

<Steps>
    <Step title="Go to the sign-up page">
        Visit <a href="https://app.shiftcontrol.io/signup" target="_blank">app.shiftcontrol.io/signup</a> and click **Sign up with Google**.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Sign in with your Google Workspace account">
        Google's sign-in screen opens. Sign in with the Google Workspace account you want to associate with your ShiftControl org. Any Workspace user can sign up at this step — you don't need to be a Super Admin yet. The Super Admin requirement applies later, at the OAuth authorization step.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Wait for domain validation">
        ShiftControl checks three things: that you signed in with a Google Workspace account (not a personal Gmail), that your email's domain is owned by your workspace, and that no existing ShiftControl org has already claimed this workspace. This usually takes a second.

        See the [Domain validation](#domain-validation) section below for what to do if you hit a validation error.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Approve permissions for ShiftControl (Super Admin required)">
        Google will show a screen asking you to grant ShiftControl permission to manage users, groups, and admin roles in your Workspace. This is the step that requires a Google Workspace **Super Admin** — other admin roles can't grant the permissions ShiftControl needs.

        If you're the Super Admin, review the list and click **Allow**. If you aren't, send a [48-hour authorization link](/using-shiftcontrol/Integrations/guides/Google-Workspace-OAuth-flow/#delegated-oauth) to a Super Admin in your organization — they can approve the connection without needing a ShiftControl account.

        ShiftControl only asks for permissions it actually uses. If you want to see exactly what each one grants and why, the [Google Workspace OAuth flow](/using-shiftcontrol/Integrations/guides/Google-Workspace-OAuth-flow/) page has the full breakdown.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Choose which app connectors to set up">
        The Connect Apps step offers HRIS integrations and other connectors. Pick the ones you want now, skip the rest. None of these are required to finish onboarding — you can come back from **Settings → Connectors** anytime.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Watch provisioning complete">
        ShiftControl reads your Google directory, creates the ShiftControl org, sets Google Workspace as your primary directory, and imports users, groups, and admin roles. Provisioning runs as a single atomic step — if anything fails, the system rolls back rather than leaving you in a half-state.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Land on Manage Users">
        Once provisioning succeeds, ShiftControl redirects you to **Users**. Your Google Workspace users, groups, departments, and admin roles are already there. From here, the [Users](/using-shiftcontrol/Users/) and [Groups](/using-shiftcontrol/Groups/) sections of the docs are your next stops.
    </Step>
</Steps>

## Domain Validation

Domain validation is where most self-service errors surface. Here's what each one means:

| Error | What it means | What to do |
|-------|---------------|------------|
| **Not a Google Workspace account** | You signed in with a `@gmail.com` or other personal Google account, not a Workspace account. | Sign out and sign in again with your work Google Workspace account. |
| **Domain not eligible** | The domain on your sign-in email isn't recognized as part of a Google Workspace. | Confirm you signed in with your Google Workspace account (not a personal Gmail) and that your domain is listed in **Google Admin → Domains**. |
| **Domain already claimed** | Another ShiftControl organization has already onboarded this workspace. | [Contact support](mailto:support@shiftcontrol.io) — usually a teammate set up the org first. We can invite you to it. |
| **Unsaved changes** | You navigated mid-onboarding and have unsaved progress. | Use the wizard back/next buttons to return to a known step. ShiftControl preserves your progress so you don't lose work. |

## Things to Know

- **You can't re-enter onboarding after it completes.** Once provisioning succeeds, the onboarding routes are gated for your org. This prevents accidentally re-running provisioning and clobbering your real data. If you genuinely need to restart, contact support.
- **Google Workspace is set as your primary directory automatically.** When provisioning runs, ShiftControl promotes Google to primary. You don't need to configure anything in **Settings → Directories** after onboarding — it's already done.
- **Provisioning is atomic.** If the Google API hits an error mid-flow, ShiftControl rolls back the partial state rather than leaving you with a half-onboarded org. Just retry.
- **You don't need to install JumpCloud.** Self-service onboarding is the Google-only path. If you later want to add JumpCloud as an additional directory, you can do that from **Settings → Connectors → Directories**.
- **You don't need to be the Super Admin yourself to start setup.** Sign up with your own Google Workspace account. At the permissions step, send a [48-hour authorization link](/using-shiftcontrol/Integrations/guides/Google-Workspace-OAuth-flow/#delegated-oauth) to your Super Admin — they can approve the connection without needing a ShiftControl account.
- **Onboarding copy and steps may shift slightly between releases.** This page describes the intended flow; minor UI label changes can land at any time without a docs update.
