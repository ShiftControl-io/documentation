## Step 1: Trust JumpCloud in Google Workspace

If you use Google Workspace alongside JumpCloud, the first step is to trust the JumpCloud apps in your Google Workspace Admin interface. This allows JumpCloud to sync users and provide Single Sign-On to Google services.

<Admonition type="note">
    This step is only required if your organization has API Controls set to **restricted** in Google Workspace Admin. However, we recommend following it regardless to ensure the apps are properly authorized.
</Admonition>

The following JumpCloud apps need to be approved:

| App | Purpose | Client ID |
|-----|---------|-----------|
| **JumpCloud** | Directory sync of users and groups | `409100557052-up2bjo6c85fdvipudtkaqr7jqsesqnam.apps.googleusercontent.com` |
| **JumpCloud** | Single Sign-On | `629652647736-b7u1qe4oktmn7b6vnmdbabrd32lds4as.apps.googleusercontent.com` |

<Steps>
    <Step title="Open Google Workspace Admin">
        Go to [admin.google.com](https://admin.google.com) and log in as a Google Workspace admin.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Navigate to API Controls">
        Go to **Security → Access and data control → API Controls**.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Open Managed Google Services">
        Click **Manage Google Services**.
        <img
            src="/img/JumpCloud-Trust/JumpCloud-Trust-Manage-Google-Services.png"
            alt="Manage Google Services in Google Workspace Admin"
        />
    </Step>
    <Step title="View configured apps">
        In the Configured Apps card, click **View list**.
        <img
            src="/img/JumpCloud-Trust/JumpCloud-Trust-Configured-Apps.png"
            alt="Configured Apps list"
        />
    </Step>
    <Step title="Add an OAuth app">
        Click the **Add App** dropdown and select **OAuth App Name or Client ID**.
        <img
            src="/img/JumpCloud-Trust/JumpCloud-Trust-Add-Oauth-App.png"
            alt="Add OAuth App"
        />
    </Step>
    <Step title="Search and approve each app">
        For each app in the table above, paste the Client ID and click **Search**. Then select the app, choose **Your Org** (all users), set it to **Trusted**, and click **Finish**.

        Repeat for both JumpCloud apps.
        <img
            src="/img/JumpCloud-Trust/JumpCloud-Trust-Search-Apps.png"
            alt="Search for JumpCloud app by Client ID"
        />
    </Step>
</Steps>

You've now authorized JumpCloud to sync with Google Workspace. Next, connect Google Workspace as a directory in ShiftControl.

## Step 2: Connect Google Workspace in ShiftControl

With JumpCloud trusted in Google, the next step is to connect Google Workspace as a directory so ShiftControl can manage both directories from one place.

<Steps>
    <Step title="Add Google Workspace from the App Catalog">
        Go to **Apps → Catalog** in ShiftControl. Search for **Google Workspace** and click **Add**. This installs Google Workspace as a managed application.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Enable the Google Workspace directory connector">
        Go to **Settings → Connectors → Directories**. Find **Google** in the list and click the **⋮ menu → Authenticate**.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Complete the authorization flow">
        A Google authorization window will open. Sign in with a Google Workspace admin account and grant ShiftControl the requested permissions. These permissions allow ShiftControl to read and manage users, groups, and admin roles in your Google Workspace.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Verify the connection">
        After authorization, the Google connector status should show **Enabled** with a **Healthy** health indicator. ShiftControl will begin syncing your Google Workspace data immediately — users, groups, and admin roles will start appearing throughout the platform.
    </Step>
</Steps>

Once connected, you'll see Google Workspace data in:
- **Users** — Directory icons showing which users exist in Google Workspace
- **Groups** — Google Groups sync and distribution list management
- **Dashboards** — OAuth permission auditing from Google Workspace
- **Settings → Google Admins** — Google Workspace admin role management

## Step 2: Start Managing Your Organization

With JumpCloud trusted (if you use Google Workspace) and ShiftControl connected to your JumpCloud directory, you're ready to start managing your organization:

1. **[Review your users](/using-shiftcontrol/Users/User-management/)** — ShiftControl syncs your JumpCloud users automatically. Check that everyone appears correctly.
2. **[Set up groups](/using-shiftcontrol/Groups/Group-management/)** — Create groups based on departments, teams, and roles to automate app access.
3. **[Manage your apps](/using-shiftcontrol/Apps/App-management/)** — Assign applications to groups so access is provisioned automatically.
