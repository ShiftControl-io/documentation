## Step 1: Trust JumpCloud in Google Workspace

If you use Google Workspace alongside JumpCloud, the first step is to trust the JumpCloud apps in your Google Workspace Admin interface. This allows JumpCloud to sync users and provide Single Sign-On to Google services.

<Admonition type="note">
    This step is only required if your organization has API Controls set to **restricted** in Google Workspace Admin. However, we recommend following it regardless to ensure the apps are properly authorized.
</Admonition>

The following JumpCloud apps need to be approved:

| App | Purpose | Client ID |
|-----|---------|-----------|
| **JumpCloud** | Directory sync of users and groups | `409100557052-up2bjo6c85fdvipudtkaqr7jqsesqnam.apps.googleusercontent.com` |
| **JumpCloud** | Single Sign-On | `629652647736-b7u1qe4oktmn7b6vnmdbabrd32lds4as.apps.googleusercontent.com` |

<Steps>
    <Step title="Open Google Workspace Admin">
        Go to [admin.google.com](https://admin.google.com) and log in as a Google Workspace admin.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Navigate to API Controls">
        Go to **Security → Access and data control → API Controls**.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Open Managed Google Services">
        Click **Manage Google Services**.
        <img
            src="/img/JumpCloud-Trust/JumpCloud-Trust-Manage-Google-Services.png"
            alt="Manage Google Services in Google Workspace Admin"
        />
    </Step>
    <Step title="View configured apps">
        In the Configured Apps card, click **View list**.
        <img
            src="/img/JumpCloud-Trust/JumpCloud-Trust-Configured-Apps.png"
            alt="Configured Apps list"
        />
    </Step>
    <Step title="Add an OAuth app">
        Click the **Add App** dropdown and select **OAuth App Name or Client ID**.
        <img
            src="/img/JumpCloud-Trust/JumpCloud-Trust-Add-Oauth-App.png"
            alt="Add OAuth App"
        />
    </Step>
    <Step title="Search and approve each app">
        For each app in the table above, paste the Client ID and click **Search**. Then select the app, choose **Your Org** (all users), set it to **Trusted**, and click **Finish**.

        Repeat for both JumpCloud apps.
        <img
            src="/img/JumpCloud-Trust/JumpCloud-Trust-Search-Apps.png"
            alt="Search for JumpCloud app by Client ID"
        />
    </Step>
</Steps>

You've now authorized JumpCloud to sync with Google Workspace. Next, connect Google Workspace as a directory in ShiftControl.

## Step 2: Connect Google Workspace in ShiftControl

With JumpCloud trusted in Google, the next step is to connect Google Workspace as a directory so ShiftControl can manage both directories from one place.

<Steps>
    <Step title="Add Google Workspace from the App Catalog">
        Go to **Apps → Catalog** in ShiftControl. Search for **Google Workspace** and click **Add**. This installs Google Workspace as a managed application.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Enable the Google Workspace directory connector">
        Go to **Settings → Connectors → Directories**. Find **Google** in the list and click the **⋮ menu → Authenticate**.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Complete the authorization flow">
        A Google authorization window will open. Sign in with a Google Workspace admin account and grant ShiftControl the requested permissions. These permissions allow ShiftControl to read and manage users, groups, and admin roles in your Google Workspace.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Verify the connection">
        After authorization, the Google connector status should show **Enabled** with a **Healthy** health indicator. ShiftControl will begin syncing your Google Workspace data immediately — users, groups, and admin roles will start appearing throughout the platform.
    </Step>
</Steps>

Once connected, you'll see Google Workspace data in:
- **Users** — Directory icons showing which users exist in Google Workspace
- **Groups** — Google Groups sync and distribution list management
- **Dashboards** — OAuth permission auditing from Google Workspace
- **Settings → Google Admins** — Google Workspace admin role management

## Step 1: Sign Up With Google Workspace

If you run on Google Workspace, you can set up your ShiftControl org in a few minutes. Sign up with your Google Workspace account, validate your domain, authorize the directory, and land on a working org — no setup call required.

<Steps>
    <Step title="Start your sign-up">
        Go to <a href="https://app.shiftcontrol.io/signup" target="_blank">app.shiftcontrol.io/signup</a> and choose **Sign up with Google**. Any Google Workspace user can start sign-up — you don't need to be a Super Admin at this step. The Super Admin requirement comes later, when ShiftControl asks to authorize the Google directory in Step 3. If you're not the Super Admin yourself, you can delegate that step.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Validate your domain">
        ShiftControl checks that the email you signed in with belongs to a Google Workspace domain you administer, and that the domain isn't already claimed by another ShiftControl org. If you hit a validation error here, see [Self-service onboarding](/getting-started/Self-service-onboarding/#domain-validation) for what each error means.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Approve permissions for ShiftControl (Super Admin required)">
        Google will show a screen asking you to grant ShiftControl permission to manage users, groups, and admin roles in your Workspace. Click **Allow** to connect the two systems.

        This is the step that needs a Google Workspace **Super Admin** — other admin roles can't grant the permissions ShiftControl needs. If you're not the Super Admin, send them a [48-hour authorization link](/using-shiftcontrol/Integrations/guides/Google-Workspace-OAuth-flow/#delegated-oauth) — they can approve the connection without needing a ShiftControl account of their own.

        If you want the technical detail on exactly what ShiftControl is being granted (and why), the [Google Workspace OAuth flow](/using-shiftcontrol/Integrations/guides/Google-Workspace-OAuth-flow/) page lists every permission with rationale.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Review discovered apps">
        ShiftControl scans your Workspace and shows you the SaaS apps your team is already signed in to. Review the list — they become your starting app catalog.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Set up departments and locations">
        Add your departments and office locations. Each one auto-creates a group with a dynamic membership rule, so users flow into the right group based on their department or location.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Start your trial">
        ShiftControl finishes provisioning and redirects you to the **Manage Users** screen. You're now in the trial. HRIS and other connectors are added anytime from **Settings → Connectors** — they're not part of onboarding.
    </Step>
</Steps>

<Admonition type="tip">
    Don't have your Google Workspace Super Admin in front of you right now? You can [send them a 48-hour authorization link](/using-shiftcontrol/Integrations/guides/Google-Workspace-OAuth-flow/#delegated-oauth) — they'll approve the connection without needing a ShiftControl account.
</Admonition>

Once setup finishes, you'll see your Google Workspace data in:
- **Users** — your full Google directory with status badges showing who needs a password reset, who's never logged in, and who hasn't accepted the Google terms of service
- **Groups** — Google Groups with automatic syncing, dynamic membership rules, and drift detection
- **Dashboards** — a view of every third-party app that has access to your Workspace and what permissions they have
- **Settings → Google Admins** — manage who holds each Google Workspace admin role
